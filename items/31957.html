Paraphrase

Sale of a boat to the head of the Egibi family and receipt of the purchase price.
A sells his boat to B for 4 minas and 10 shekels of unmarked silver, of which 1/8 is alloy, ša halla iṣṣur*. The seller assumes warranty for the uškûtu of his boat. He receives payment (eṭēru) for the purchase price (šīmu), namely the agreed upon amount of 4 minas and 10 shekels, from the buyer. The boat is a cargo-boat (rukubbu), 6 ½ cubīts wide at its beam (šaburru), with a keel? (šubat mê) of 20 cubīts, covered (kuttummu) with mats (šû), and loaded (elû Š) with cress!? (sahlû).** Names of 5 witnesses and the scribe.

*The meaning of the latter idiom is unknown (cf. CAD H, 43 s.v. halla).

A = Nabû-aplu-iddin/Lâbāši//Nūr-Papsukkal; B = Širik (wr. mši-rik-ki)/Iddinaya//Egibi (=Marduk-nāṣir-apli/Itti-Marduk-balāṭu//Egibi)
