Texts from the database reflect how daily existence by the rivers of Babylon was a constant struggle to get access to the canals that irrigated the fields. Even though Babylonian elite society in this period was centered in urban areas, subsistence was primarily agricultural and the villages in the hinterland were the economic backbone of the cities and temples.
